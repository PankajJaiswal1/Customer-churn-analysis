# Telco Customer Churn Analysis & Retention Insights

## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer churn behavior in a telecommunications company to identify key factors influencing customer retention.  
The analysis focuses on contract types, payment methods, customer tenure, internet service type, and demographic attributes to understand why customers leave and how churn can be reduced.

The goal is to provide data-driven insights that support effective customer retention strategies.

---

## Business Problem
Customer churn directly impacts revenue and long-term business growth in the telecom industry.  
Understanding which customer segments are more likely to churn helps organizations design targeted retention programs, improve customer experience, and reduce revenue loss.

This project addresses the challenge by analyzing historical customer data to uncover churn patterns and risk factors.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze churn behavior across different contract types  
- Evaluate the impact of payment methods on churn  
- Study churn trends based on customer tenure  
- Identify high-risk customer segments  
- Provide actionable retention recommendations  

---

## Dataset Description
The dataset contains customer-level information including:
- Contract type and tenure  
- Payment methods  
- Internet service type  
- Demographic indicators (senior citizen status)  
- Churn status

## Methodology
1. Loaded and cleaned customer churn data using Pandas  
2. Performed exploratory data analysis to identify churn trends  
3. Calculated churn rates across contract types, payment methods, and tenure groups  
4. Visualized churn patterns using bar plots and line charts  
5. Interpreted results to derive business insights and recommendations  

---

## Key Insights
- Customers on **month-to-month contracts show a 42% churn rate**, compared to **11% for one-year** and **3% for two-year contracts**  
- Customers using **electronic check payment methods have the highest churn rate (45%)**, while other payment methods average **15â€“18%**  
- Customers with **less than one year of tenure have a 50% churn rate**, which drops to **15% after three years**  
- **Fiber optic users churn at 30%**, compared to **20% for DSL users**  
- **Senior citizens (65+) have a higher churn rate (41%)** than non-senior customers  

---

## Business Recommendations
- Encourage customers to shift to long-term contracts through incentives  
- Promote stable payment methods to reduce churn risk  
- Focus retention efforts on customers in their first year  
- Improve service experience for fiber optic users  
- Design targeted retention programs for senior citizens
